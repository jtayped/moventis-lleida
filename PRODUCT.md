# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lleida residents and daily commuters checking real-time bus arrival times, typically on a mobile device while standing at a stop. The primary context is outdoor, on-the-go, often under sun glare or with one hand occupied. They want one thing: when is my bus arriving.

## Product Purpose

A fast, clean alternative to the official Moventis site for viewing real-time and scheduled bus arrivals in Lleida. The official site is slow and hard to use on mobile; this tool replaces that experience with a map-first interface that shows arrivals for any stop in seconds. Success is measured by how quickly a user gets from "open app" to "I know when my bus arrives."

## Brand Personality

Modern, lightweight, civic. Feels like city infrastructure that actually got updated — precise and useful, without the institutional weight. Honest about being a community-built tool. Never decorative for its own sake.

## Anti-references

- **Transit agency branding**: Government-style design — heavy institutional colors, overly formal type, high-contrast block structure. This is what we are reacting against.
- **Generic SaaS**: Blue primary everywhere, hero stats, identical rounded card grids, "seamless" copy. Could be any startup product.
- **The official Moventis site**: Cluttered, slow, difficult to navigate on mobile.

## Design Principles

1. **Information over chrome**: Every pixel of UI should serve the data. If removing an element doesn't break the user's ability to read arrival times, it shouldn't be there.
2. **Mobile-outdoor first**: Designed for a phone in sunlight at a bus stop. Tap targets are generous, text is readable without squinting, contrast holds in high ambient light.
3. **Civic without institutional**: Public-service usefulness with modern restraint. Approachable and local, not bureaucratic or corporate.
4. **Let the lines speak**: Per-line transit colors are the primary visual identity. Brand color anchors chrome and interactive elements; it never competes with line colors.
5. **Speed signals trust**: Instant perceived feedback — loading states, live countdown timers, refresh animations — reassures the user standing at the stop that the data is current.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum across all interactive elements. Mobile-outdoor optimizations: large tap targets (minimum 44px), high-contrast mode support, legible in direct sunlight (avoid low-chroma grays for functional text). Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations.
